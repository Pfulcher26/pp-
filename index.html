Why Programming?

There is a short sonnet by Japan's most famous Haiku poet Basho that goes, "Even in Kyoto / Hearing the Cuckoo's Cry/ I long for Kyoto". Throughout my life I have longed, like Basho for the ephemeral Kyoto.

'The Kid's Got Heart'

In conclusion, I want to take a moment to think about the heart kid from Captain Planet. Yes, I don't even know the heart kid's name. Okay, I just googled it, praise be. It's Ma-Ti. So, Ma-Ti, as is commonly conferred upon, always seemed like the lamest planeteer. He couldn't really do anything. While everyone else was out moving lava, creating tornados, splitting the earth in two, Ma-Ti was out making friends with monkeys. But from the current perspective, as one grows up, and enters into, becomes part of the world, and views it on its own terms, we see that Ma-Ti, as hackneyed as it sounds, was actually the most essential of all the planeteers, because he had possessed the power of the only element within the world that we as humans can control: the heart. As programmers, we are much the same. Though we manipulate extremely powerful tools now capable of altering, and in some cases superceding reality, our truest power lies in how we care for the world, and our place in it. We must strive to possess the power of Ma-Ti. We must strive to be, now and always, refreshing human-beings.
